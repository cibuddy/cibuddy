CIBuddy by cibuddy.com

Visualization Project for Continuous Integration Servers

What is it about?

CI Servers like Jenkins or Hudson are playing a crucial role in our daily business and are essential for high quality software. Unfortunately, we're living in a fast-paced world and we are often overwhelmed with information coming from many different sources like instant messengers, email or text messages for instance. So it is no surprise that the actual status of the our build environment and therefore the quality of our software tends to lack some love and attention.

Here is where CIBuddy comes into play. With CIBuddy you are able to bind physical build indicators like USB lights or geeky stuff like the iBuddy to the status of your various build server projects. Just configure what project status should reflect what kind of physical reaction of your lamp (or tool) - that easy. Now you always have the status of your important projects right infront of your eyes and no longer have excuses for not maintaining a fast feedback on build problems. You immediately see when something breaks one of your important projects.

Usage

The usage is as easy as it can get. This is what you need:

  • the binary of CIBuddy
  • a list of your build servers (currently only jenkins is supported)
  • a physical build indicator lamp (like the iBuddy or a delcom light)

Extending CIBuddy

CIBuddy is implemented in Java and is based on OSGi. It is developed with the idea to be easily extendable and as such contains many extension points that make it very easy to start working. Just have a look at the github page and read through the documentation to learn more about it.
